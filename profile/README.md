# 🛡️ Cyber Threat Hunting Playground

**Cyber Threat Hunting Playground** is a GitHub **organization** dedicated to **centralizing and sharing Cyber Threat Hunting resources** across multiple repositories.

This organization hosts **tools, scripts, methodologies, playbooks, and research projects**—either created from scratch or derived from forked and adapted external repositories—to support proactive cyber defense and threat hunting activities.

***

## 🎯 Mission & Vision

The purpose of this organization is to:

*   ✅ Centralize **Threat Hunting knowledge and tooling**
*   ✅ Encourage **hands-on experimentation and learning**
*   ✅ Share **custom-built and community-adapted hunting resources**
*   ✅ Promote **methodology-driven threat hunting**
*   ✅ Foster **collaboration among hunters and blue teamers**

Rather than a single monolithic repository, this organization acts as a **hub**, grouping multiple focused repositories under a common Threat Hunting vision.

***

## 📦 What You’ll Find Here

Repositories within this organization may cover a wide range of hunting-related topics, including but not limited to:

### 🔧 Tools & Automation

*   Standalone hunting tools
*   Python, PowerShell, and Bash scripts
*   EDR / SIEM helper utilities
*   Data enrichment and analysis tooling

### 🔍 Hunting Queries & Detections

*   Hunting queries for SIEMs and data lakes  
    (KQL, SPL, SQL, etc.)
*   Behavioral detection experiments
*   MITRE ATT\&CK–mapped hunt ideas

### 📚 Methodologies & Playbooks

*   Hypothesis-driven hunting approaches
*   Investigation workflows
*   Playbooks and operational guidance

### 🌐 Research & References

*   Forked external repositories adapted for hunting
*   Proofs of concept inspired by public research
*   Documentation derived from vendor or open-source material

Each repository is **self-contained**, with its own scope, README, and documentation.

***

## 🧭 How to Use This Organization

*   Browse repositories by **topic or tool**
*   Clone only what you need for your environment
*   Use the resources as **inspiration**, **learning material**, or **building blocks**
*   Adapt and improve them for your own hunting workflows

This is a **playground**, not a turnkey product.
